Dog-friendly hikes in Val Gardena

Eight trails in and around the valley, scored the DoloPaws way: for paws, shade, water and terrain, not just for views.

Val Gardena is one of the friendliest Dolomites valleys to visit with a dog, and not by accident: the valley publishes official rules for dog owners, all its cable cars and chairlifts carry dogs (ticket, leash and muzzle required), and the villages are connected by a nearly flat path along an old railway line that might be the best warm-up walk in the Dolomites. Above the valley, though, the terrain grades fast: from meadow strolls to scree circuits that most dogs shouldn't attempt. Here's the honest ranking.

Valley rules in one paragraph: leash mandatory, muzzle carried at all times (worn on lifts and buses), EU pet passport with rabies vaccination on you, and dog tickets for lifts. Full details in our guide to taking your dog on cable cars, and check the official Val Gardena rules. Individual municipalities can add their own.

Easy days, any dog, any age

Sentiero del Trenino Low-risk 🐾 Verified

12.4 km · 462 m gain · ~3.5 h · Ortisei – Santa Cristina – Selva

The old narrow-gauge railway line (1916–1960) turned walking path, connecting all three villages with historical info boards and a walkable restored tunnel near Santa Cristina. Officially rated easy, low heat risk, and walkable in sections. Take the bus back from any village. The best first-day walk in the valley with a dog.

Alpe di Siusi Meadow Loop Low-risk 🐾 Verified

7.5 km · 150 m gain · 2–2.5 h · Alpe di Siusi (next valley over)

Europe's largest high-alpine meadow, on wide gravel and paved paths gentle enough for all-terrain strollers. One caveat that matters for dogs: shade is scarce (about 20%), so on hot days go early. Grazing herds use these meadows in summer: leash up and read our guardian dog guide first.

Sentiero Hans e Paula Steger Low-risk 🐾 Verified

8.7 km · 233 m gain · 2.5–3 h · Compaccio to Saltria, Alpe di Siusi

Wide, easy and mostly flat across the Alpe di Siusi with Sassolungo filling the horizon. Independently confirmed dog-friendly. Lift access from the valley means a dog ticket and muzzle: see the rules above.

Moderate days, fit dogs, planned heat

Seceda Ridge Trail Moderate 🐾 Verified

8 km · 400 m gain · 2.5–4 h · via Ortisei–Furnes gondola (paid)

The famous ridge line above the valley, with rest areas and water along the way. The honest caveats: rocky steps near the ridge, roughly 10% shade, and a high heat risk in summer. Early gondola, full water bottles, and this becomes one of the great dog days in the Dolomites. Flat-faced breeds should sit this one out on hot days: here's why.

Geotrail Bula / Pufels / Bulla Moderate 🐾 Verified

8.8 km · 340 m gain · 2.5–3 h · Passo Pinei to Bulla

A geological education trail where you physically cross the 252-million-year-old Permian–Triassic boundary, with 8 info panels on how the Dolomites formed. Confirmed dog-friendly by multiple independent guides (leashed; off-leash permitted in some areas), with decent shade for the area.

Giro del Bulacia Moderate 🐾 Verified

6.6 km · ~2.5–3 h (estimated) · Bulaccia, above the Alpe di Siusi

A marked loop past Tschötsch Alm, Rifugio Puflatsch and the Panche delle Streghe viewpoint, confirmed dog-friendly by a first-hand account of the route walked with a dog. Some figures for this route are estimates rather than source-verified: we say so on the trail page rather than inventing precision.

Big days, read the warnings first

Giro del Sasso Lungo Moderate 🐾 Verified

18.2 km · 5–6 h (estimated) · Passo Sella

The full circuit of the Sassolungo group at valley-rim level. The distance alone is the filter here: 18 km is a serious ask for most dogs, and far more strides for short legs. Plan rifugio stops, carry water, and be ready to turn it into an out-and-back.

Sassolungo–Sassopiatto Loop Caution 🐾 Verified

14 km · 750 m gain · 5–7 h · Passo Sella (paid lift access)

A magnificent route we rate Caution for dogs, and we mean it: scree fields, long exposed stretches, about 10% shade and high heat risk. Sharp scree is exactly the surface that wears pads down fast. For most dogs the Giro or the Steger path is the better version of this day. If you go: booties, early start, and turn-back discipline.

Which of these fits your dog?

The ratings above describe the mountain. The same for every dog. A Corgi, a Bernese and a Vizsla will experience Seceda's 400 m of gain very differently.
